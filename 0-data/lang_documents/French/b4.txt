French words: (English translation, English pronunciation)

* Merci (mer-see): Thank you
* Merci beaucoup (mer-see bow-koo): Thank you very much
* Merci infiniment (mer-see an-fee-nee-man): Thank you infinitely
* C'est très gentil à toi/vous (seh treh zhan-tee ah twa/voo): That's very kind of you
* Je vous remercie (zhuh voo re-mer-see): I thank you (formal)

Tips:

* Which phrase to use

The phrase you choose to thank someone will depend on the context and your relationship with the person you are thanking. For example, if you are thanking a friend or family member for a small favor, you can say "Merci". If you are thanking a stranger or someone for a more significant gesture, you can say "Merci beaucoup" or "Merci infiniment".

* Pronunciation

The pronunciation of French thank-you phrases is relatively straightforward. Here are a few tips:

* The "j" in "Merci" is pronounced like the "s" in "measure".
* The "r" in "Merci" is pronounced in the back of the throat.
* The "s" in "Merci beaucoup" is silent.

* Sincerity

When thanking someone in French, it is important to be sincere. Make eye contact and smile, and use a genuine tone of voice.

Specific tip:

If you are unsure which phrase to use, it is always safe to say "Merci beaucoup". It is a polite and versatile expression of gratitude that can be used in any situation.

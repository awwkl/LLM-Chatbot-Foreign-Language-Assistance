Here are some basic French phrases for beginners:

* Bonjour (bon-zhoor): Hello
* Bonsoir (bon-swahr): Good evening
* Au revoir (oh-re-vwar): Goodbye
* Merci (mer-see): Thank you
* S'il vous plaît (sil-voo-play): Please
* Excusez-moi (ex-koo-zay-mwah): Excuse me
* Oui (wee): Yes
* Non (noh): No
* Je ne comprends pas (zhuh nuh comp-ron pah): I don't understand
* Parlez-vous anglais ? (par-lay-voo ahn-glay ?) Do you speak English?
* Je m'appelle [your name] (zhuh mah-pell): My name is
* Comment ça va ? (comm-ahn sah vah ?) How are you?
* Bien, merci. Et vous ? (byahn, mer-see. ay voo ?) Good, thanks. And you?

Tips for learning French:

* Immerse yourself in the language as much as possible. This means surrounding yourself with French speakers and French media. You can watch French movies and TV shows, listen to French music, and read French books and articles.
* Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Everyone makes mistakes when they're learning a new language. The important thing is to keep practicing.
* Find a language partner or take a French class. This is a great way to get regular practice speaking and listening to French.
* Don't give up! Learning a new language takes time and effort, but it's definitely possible.

Specific tips for the question you asked:

* When greeting someone in French, it's important to use the correct greeting for the time of day. "Bonjour" is used in the morning and early afternoon, "bonsoir" is used in the evening, and "au revoir" is used when saying goodbye.
* If you don't understand something someone says, don't be afraid to ask them to repeat it. You can say "Excusez-moi, pouvez-vous répéter, s'il vous plaît ?" (ex-koo-zay-mwah, poo-vey-voo ray-pay-tay, sil-voo-play ?)
* If you're not sure which word to use, you can always ask someone for help. You can say "Comment dit-on '...' en français ?" (comm-ahn dee-ton '...' ahn frahn-say ?)
* The most important thing is to practice speaking and listening to French as much as possible.

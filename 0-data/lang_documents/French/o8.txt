Here are some examples of how to negotiate or bargain the price in French, for someone new to the French language:

French words: Quel est votre meilleur prix ? (What is your best price?)
Pronunciation: kel ay luh vo-truh mee-yuh pree?

French words: Pouvez-vous baisser le prix ? (Can you lower the price?)
Pronunciation: poo-vay-voo bay-say luh pree?

French words: C'est trop cher. Pouvez-vous faire un effort ? (It's too expensive. Can you make an effort?)
Pronunciation: say troh shehr. poo-vay-voo fayr un eh-for?

French words: Je suis prêt(e) à acheter maintenant si vous me faites un bon prix. (I'm ready to buy now if you give me a good price.)
Pronunciation: zhuh swee preh-t(e) ah sheh-tay may-ntahn see vooz mee fayt un bon pree.

French words: Je vais réfléchir. Mais je pense que je peux trouver le même produit moins cher ailleurs. (I'll think about it. But I think I can find the same product cheaper elsewhere.)
Pronunciation: zhuh vay re-fle-sheer. may zhuh pahn-s kuh zhuh pwah troo-vay luh maym proh-dwee muhng sherr ah-yuh.

Tips for negotiating or bargaining the price in French:

* Be polite. Use the polite form of the verbs "pouvoir" (to be able to), "baisser" (to lower), and "faire" (to make).
* Be specific about what you want. If you are asking for a discount, for example, say "Pouvez-vous me faire une remise de 10% ?" (Can you give me a 10% discount?)
* Be prepared to walk away if you are not happy with the price. This shows the seller that you are serious about negotiating.

Additional tips:

* If you are not sure how to say something in French, you can try to describe it. For example, if you don't know how to say "discount", you could say "une réduction" (a reduction).
* If you are asking for a discount on a multiple item purchase, you could say "Pouvez-vous me faire un prix spécial pour ces deux articles ?" (Can you give me a special price for these two items?)
* Be patient. It may take some time to negotiate a good price.

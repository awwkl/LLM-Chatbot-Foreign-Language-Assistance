How to introduce yourself in French (for beginners)

French words: Je m'appelle John. Je viens de [insert your country].

English translation: My name is John. I am from [insert your country].

English pronunciation: zhuh mah-pell zhon. zhuh vee-en duh [insert your country].

Examples:

* Je m'appelle John. Je viens des États-Unis. (My name is John. I am from the United States.)
* Je m'appelle John. Je viens du Royaume-Uni. (My name is John. I am from the United Kingdom.)
* Je m'appelle John. Je viens du Canada. (My name is John. I am from Canada.)
* Je m'appelle John. Je viens de France. (My name is John. I am from France.)

Tips for introducing yourself in French:

* When introducing yourself, it is customary to say your last name first.
* If you are speaking to a group of people, you can say "Je m'appelle John et je viens de [insert your country]." (My name is John and I am from [insert your country]).
* If you are unsure of your pronunciation, you can ask the other person to repeat it.

Specific tips for the question you asked:

* If you are introducing yourself to someone for the first time, you can say "Enchanté(e)." (Nice to meet you.)
* If you are introducing yourself in a professional setting, you can say "Bonjour, je m'appelle John et je suis [insert your job title]." (Hello, my name is John and I am a [insert your job title].)

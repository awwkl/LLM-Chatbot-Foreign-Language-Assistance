To negotiate or bargain the price in Mandarin, you can use the following phrases:

* 便宜一点。 (piányí yīdiǎn) - A little cheaper.
* 太贵了。 (tài guìle) - Too expensive.
* 最低多少钱？ (zuìdī duōshǎo qián?) - What's the lowest price?
* 如果我多买几个，你能便宜一点吗？ (rúguǒ wǒ duō mǎi jǐgè, nǐ néng piányí yīdiǎn ma?) - If I buy more, can you give me a discount?
* 我可以去别的地方买，那里比较便宜。 (wǒ kěyǐ qù bié de dìfāng mǎi, nàlǐ bǐjiào piányí.) - I can buy this somewhere else for cheaper.

Pronunciation:

* 便宜一点。 (piányí yīdiǎn) - piān-yí yī-diǎn
* 太贵了。 (tài guìle) - tài guì-le
* 最低多少钱？ (zuìdī duōshǎo qián?) - zuì-dī duō-shǎo qián?
* 如果我多买几个，你能便宜一点吗？ (rúguǒ wǒ duō mǎi jǐgè, nǐ néng piányí yīdiǎn ma?) - rú-guǒ wǒ duō mǎi jǐ-gè, nǐ néng piányí yī-diǎn ma?
* 我可以去别的地方买，那里比较便宜。 (wǒ kěyǐ qù bié de dìfāng mǎi, nàlǐ bǐjiào piányí.) - wǒ kě-yǐ qù bié de dì-fāng mǎi, nà-lǐ bǐ-jiào piān-yí.

Tips:

* Be polite and respectful when you're negotiating.
* Be prepared to walk away if you don't get a good deal.
* Don't be afraid to ask for a discount, especially if you're buying multiple items.
* Do some research on the product or service you're interested in before you start negotiating. This will give you a better idea of what a fair price is.

Example:

* 我很喜欢这件衣服，但是太贵了。你能便宜一点吗？ (wǒ hěn xǐhuān zhè jiàn yīfú, dànshì tài guìle. nǐ néng piányí yīdiǎn ma?) - I really like this shirt, but it's too expensive. Can you give me a discount?

Here are some ways to order food in Spanish, for someone new to the Spanish language:

Spanish words: (English translation, English pronunciation)

* Hola, ¿me puede traer el menú?: Hello, can I please have the menu? (OH-lah, ¿meh pwEH-deh ah-yoo-DAH el meh-NOO?)
* ¿Qué me recomienda?: What do you recommend? (¿Keh meh reh-koh-MYEN-dah?)
* Quisiera pedir el...: I would like to order the... (kee-SYEH-rah pee-DEEHR el...)
* Para mí, el...: For me, the... (PAH-rah mee, el...)
* ¿Con qué lo/la quiere?: What would you like it with? (¿Kon keh loh/lah KYEH-reh?)
* ¿Cómo lo/la quiere?: How would you like it? (¿KOH-moh loh/lah KYEH-reh?)
* La cuenta, por favor.: The bill, please. (Lah KWEH-n-tah, por fah-BOHR.)

Tips for ordering food in Spanish:

* Be polite and respectful.
* Use the appropriate form of address, depending on the person you are speaking to. For example, you would use "usted" (formal you) when speaking to someone older than you or someone you don't know well. You would use "tú" (informal you) when speaking to friends or family.
* Be specific about what you want to order. For example, instead of saying "Quiero un plato de pollo", say "Quiero un plato de pollo asado con arroz y verduras" (I want a plate of roasted chicken with rice and vegetables).
* If you are not sure how to say something in Spanish, don't be afraid to ask for help. Most waiters and waitresses are happy to help tourists.

Here are some additional tips:

* If you are ordering food for a group, let the waiter or waitress know how many people you are ordering for.
* If you have any dietary restrictions, such as allergies or food intolerances, be sure to tell the waiter or waitress.
* If you are unsure of what to order, ask the waiter or waitress for recommendations.
* Be patient and understanding. It may take the waiter or waitress a few minutes to take your order and serve your food.
